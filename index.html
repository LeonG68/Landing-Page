The Process

10 months ago, a EUV machine broke down in an Intel fab. The lost printing time costed Intel around $100,000 per hour. Engineers in the US and Netherlands worked around the clock trying to bring the machine back to production, only to fail at every attempt. A week later, the machine is finally back up, costing Intel millions of dollars in lost production time. In the post mortum analysis, we later find out that the exact problem occured in Taiwan.

Determined to solve this problem, I collaborated with our knowledge management and IT department. We dove into our existing systems and solutions and external tools. We interviewed friends at many different companies and generated a portfolio of ideas.


Slowly, we refined our portfolio of ideas and identified the ones that were technically feasible, which we combined into the current version of our product, Cognizant.

Knowledge Capture

The user's screen and audio is captured and transcribed into text using Google's Vision Api and Wav2Vec2. We protect user privacy with a neural network that automatically removes personal or non-essential information.

  • Vision API
  • Wav2Vec2
  • Privacy Filter

Topic Modeling and Generation

The transcribed text is then embedded into higher dimension space with natural language processing and clustered by similarities. Each cluster is assigned a topic by using c-TF-IDF

  • Learn more

Knowledge Graph Generation

A knowledge graph is automatically created based on the transcribed text. The database will keep tabs on individual, team, and organizational knowledge graphs.

  • Learn more

Fine-Tuning GPT-J

Users can fine tune GPT-J on organization specific topics created through topic modeling and generation. LoRa greatly reduces the computational cost of fine-tuning, allowing users to experiment freely and create specialized models.

  • GPT-J
  • LoRa

Recommendation Systems

Through user studies, we found that individuals often do not know what to search or have the correct attention triggers to know when to search. Our recommendation system will automate search by analyzing the recorded knowledge and bring the best information to the user.

Applications

Learn how our product will break down silos, help generate ideas, aid user development,and increase efficency.

Problem and Projects Recommendations

Recommending the best problems and projects in the organization that the user could solve or advise, and recommending similar problems and projects previously solved or in progress that the user can learn from.

Expert Recommendations

Automatically find the best individuals within the company for the user to network with. The individuals could be working on related problems, have overlapping knowledge, or be a good fit for a project.

Individual Development

Utilizing our individual and team knowledge graphs to identify best directions for individual development while finding knowledge gaps to fill.

Document Recommendations

Recommending the relavent but undiscovered documents.

Fine Tuned GPT-J

Fine tuned large language models that efficently retrieve knowledge through question and answering, autocomplete, and text classification.

Automatic Taxonomy and Classifcation

Topic modeling and generation creates an automatic taxonomy system that can be fine tuned with a human in the loop approach. Future knowledge will be automatically classified according to the selected taxonomy.

Oversight

Utilizes knowledge graphs, recent documents, social graphs, and differences to give key individuals such as knowledge managers/experts and architects a birds eye view of the organization.

Anomaly Detection

Analyzes the user's knowledge behavior to either identify security risks or strides towards innovation.
